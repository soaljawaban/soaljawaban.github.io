8. cara mencari lebar pada baloknote :ada yang main fifa mobile gaknama akunku anassr ovr 114​


Jawaban:

Jika yang diketahui ada volumenya maka menggunakan rumus:

l = V : (p x t)

Jika yang diketahui ada luas permukaannya maka menggunakan rumus:

l = (Lp : 2 – p x t) : p + t

Ket:

l = Lebar

p = Panjang

t = Tinggi

V = Volume

LP = Luas permukaan

Penjelasan dengan langkah-langkah:

Kita Coba Cara mengerjakan rumusnya:

Soal 1. Pak Husen mempunyai sebuah kotak kardus yang memiliki volume 400 cm³ dan memiliki panjang 16 cm serta tinggi 10 cm. Tentukan lebar kotak kardus tersebut!

Yang diketahui: 1. Volumenya (V): 400 cm³

2. Panjang 16 cm

3. Tinggi 10 cm

Penyelesaiannya:

l = V : (p x t)

l = 400 : (16 x 10)

l = 400 : 160

l = 2,5 cm.

Jadi Lebar kardus tersebut 2,5 Cm

Soal 2. Sebuah toko market terdapat keranjang buah yang memiliki luas permukaan 770 cm². Jika panjang balok 21 cm dan tingginya 7 cm, Berapa lebar keranjang buah tersebut?

Yang diketahui: 1. Luas permukaan (Lp): 770 cm²

2. Panjang 21 cm

3. Tinggi 7 cm

Penyelesaiannya:

l = (L : 2 – p x t) : p + t

l = (770 : 2 – 21 x 7) : 21 + 7

l = (385 – 147) : 28

l = 238 : 28

l = 8,5 cm

Jadi Lebar keranjang buah tersebut 8,5 cm.

36. 1. Why does mobile phone user increase dramatically in recent years? 2. What the function of mobile phone? 3. Why students not allowed bring their phone in school? 4. What are potentially lead less concentration in the time of learning and teaching process?. 5. What is the benefit of small size of the cell phone?​


Jawaban:

1. The increase in mobile phone users in recent years can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the advancement of technology has led to the development of more affordable and accessible mobile phones, making them available to a larger population. Additionally, the widespread availability of internet connectivity and mobile apps has made mobile phones essential for various purposes such as communication, information access, social media, entertainment, and productivity. The convenience and versatility offered by mobile phones have also contributed to their popularity.

2. The function of a mobile phone goes beyond simple communication. Today's mobile phones serve multiple purposes. They allow users to make calls, send text messages, and access the internet. Mobile phones also function as personal organizers, cameras, music players, GPS navigators, and gaming devices. They provide access to various apps and services for productivity, social networking, entertainment, banking, shopping, and more. Additionally, mobile phones offer features such as email, video calling, voice assistants, and online messaging platforms, enabling efficient and convenient communication.

3. Schools may have policies that restrict or prohibit students from bringing their phones to maintain a focused learning environment. Some reasons for this restriction include:

a. Distractions: Mobile phones can be distracting for students. They may be tempted to engage in activities like texting, browsing social media, or playing games, which can divert their attention from the lesson and disrupt the learning process.

b. Academic Integrity: Mobile phones can be used for cheating during exams or assessments. By restricting their use, schools aim to ensure fairness and maintain the integrity of the assessment process.

c. Cyberbullying and Misuse: Mobile phones can be misused for cyberbullying, spreading inappropriate content, or engaging in other harmful activities. Schools may implement phone restrictions to create a safe and inclusive learning environment.

d. Social Interaction: Limiting phone use during school hours encourages face-to-face interaction among students, fostering social skills, collaboration, and active participation in classroom activities.

4. Several factors can potentially lead to a lack of concentration during the learning and teaching process:

a. Distractions: Mobile phones, as well as other electronic devices and external stimuli, can divert students' attention and disrupt their concentration on the lesson.

b. Fatigue: Lack of sleep, tiredness, or physical discomfort can impact concentration and focus during the learning process.

c. Lack of Interest: When students are not engaged or interested in the subject matter, they may struggle to concentrate and retain information.

d. Environmental Factors: Noise, overcrowded classrooms, uncomfortable seating, or inadequate lighting can create distractions and hinder concentration.

e. Personal Factors: Individual factors such as stress, anxiety, or personal issues can affect a student's ability to concentrate on their studies.

5. The small size of a cell phone offers several benefits:

a. Portability: The small size makes cell phones highly portable and easy to carry in pockets or bags, allowing users to have constant access to communication and various functionalities wherever they go.

b. Convenience: The compact size of cell phones enables one-handed operation, making it easier to handle and use in different situations.

c. Discreetness: The small size allows cell phones to be discreetly used in public places without drawing excessive attention.

d. Accessibility: The small size makes cell phones more accessible to people with limited mobility or dexterity, as they can be operated with one hand and require less physical effort.

e. Design Options: The small size offers design flexibility, allowing manufacturers to create sleek and compact devices with a range of features and functionalities.

It's important to note that while the small size of cell phones offers benefits, it also has limitations, such as smaller screens and potential difficulty for some users, particularly those with visual impairments or difficulties
