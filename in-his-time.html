14. Mr. Goh is 5 times as old as his son. In 8 years time, the sum of their ages will be 68. How old will his son be in 8 years time.


Jawab:

Let's denote the son's current age as "x." According to the given information, Mr. Goh is 5 times as old as his son, so his current age can be expressed as "5x."

In 8 years, the son's age will be x + 8, and Mr. Goh's age will be 5x + 8. The sum of their ages in 8 years will be 68, so we can write the equation:

(x + 8) + (5x + 8) = 68

Combining like terms, we have:

6x + 16 = 68

Subtracting 16 from both sides:

6x = 52

Dividing both sides by 6:

x = 8.67

Since the son's age cannot be a fraction, we can round it to the nearest whole number. Therefore, the son's current age is approximately 9 years.

In 8 years, the son's age will be 9 + 8 = 17 years.

23. Unaisah likes cycling in his spare time,... Fatimah (use either,neitheir , so, or too)


Explanation:

So and Too are used in a positive sentence. In Indonesia language, they mean "juga". They are used to connect 2 clauses/sentences which show positive agreement. So is placed at the front of a clause, while too is placed at the end of the clause.
The structure (for simple present tense):
SO: So + Auxiliary (do/does/is/am/are/have/has) + subject
TOO: S + Auxiliary + too

For example:
She likes orange juice
I like orange juice
These two clauses can be combined into one sentence. It will be:
She likes orange juice, so do i.
OR
She likes orange juice, I do too.

Neither and either are used in a negative sentence. In Indonesia language, they also mean "juga". They are used to connect 2 clauses/sentences which show negative agreement. Neither is placed at the front of a clause, while either is placed at the end of the clause.
The structure:
Neither: Neither + Auxiliary + S
Either: S + Auxiliary + NOT + Either

For example:
I don't read a novel
Rina doesn't read a novel
It will be:
I don't read a novel, neither does Rina
OR
I don't read a novel, Rina does not either.

Answer:
By looking at the structure of the sentence we can conclude that the blank part uses SO. Because the sentence is a positive sentence and the blank part position is at the front. So, It will be:
Unaisah likes cycling in his spare time, So does Fatimah.
