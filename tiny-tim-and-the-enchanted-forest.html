33.  find example of suggestions and offer in the story"The Enchanted Fish" at least five


The Enchanted Fish is a story written by Brother's Grimm. We can find several examples of suggestion and offer in this story. In order to find sentences that indicate suggestion and offer, there are some different samples of expressions for suggestion and offer which you can use to find at least five suggestion and offer in the story.

Discussion

Suggestion and offer are very similar. Both suggestion and offer are ideas that we give to others for consideration. People can accept or reject the idea we give to them. A sentence that indicates a suggestion/offer usually uses the modal verb 'should' and 'shall'. Another common phrases that we can use for a suggestion/offer are 'what about + verb -ing', 'how about + verb -ing'. Here are some examples of a suggestion and offer.

- I think you should go to the headmaster office as soon as possible.

- What about working on our presentation right after we finish our lunch?

- Shall I ask the director on your behalf?

- May I help you?

- Would you like me to help?

- Would you like some drink?

Answers

We can find some examples of offers and suggestions from the story. Please take a look at some of these sentences below.

1. When the man return home, his wife asked him if he caught anything. The man said he caught a flounder but then released him upon hearing that the fish is an enchanted prince. After that, his wife suggested him to ask for a nice cottage. Here's her suggestion:

"You should have asked for a little cottage for us. Go back and call him. Tell him that we want to have a little cottage. He will surely give it to us." - The man's wife.

2. When the man return to their cottage, the wife offered him to go inside together.

"Come in. See, now isn't this much better?" - The wife.

3. The man saw that they have a beautiful cottage now with a beautiful yard full of chickens, ducks, and a garden with vegetables and fruits.

"Look," said the wife. "Isn't this nice?"

4. After getting the cottage just as the flounder said, the wife the suggested another idea to the man.

"The flounder could have given us a larger house. I would like to live in a large stone palace. Go back to the flounder and tell him to give us a palace." - The man's wife.

5. The wife seemed to be not satisfied with the cottage so she suggested another idea to her husband. She wanted a palace.

"Listen, husband. This cottage is too small. The yard and the garden are too little. The flounder could have given us a larger house. I would like to live in a large stone palace. Go back to the flounder and tell him to give us a palace."


Conclusion

The story is full of suggestion from the wife. You can read the story and find another examples on it. The examples that I wrote are only samples that you can use for a reference. Good luck!
