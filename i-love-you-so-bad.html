29. Back To School - Tolong bantu jawab ya. X: Why were you so nervous at the interview? Y: I was ... a. Such bad prepared b. So bad prepared c. So badly prepared d. Such badly prepared e. Such a bad preparation


Answer :
I was so badly prepared. (C)

Explanation :
Adverbs of degree So and Such
So is placed before an adjective/adjective phrase.
e.g.
He is so tall.
She was so highly praised.

Such is placed before a noun phrase (adjective/adverb + noun)
e.g.
He is such a tall man.
She was such a highly praised actress.
They are such smart students.

'prepared' is an adjective. To modify an adjective, we use an adverb. The adverb of 'bad' is 'badly'.

'badly prepared' is an adjective phrase. To modify an adjective phrases, use 'such'.
