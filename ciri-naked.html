An aurora is a natural light display in the sky particularly in the latitude (Artic and Antartica) regions, caused by the collision of energetic charged particles with atoms in the high latitude atmosphere (thermosphere). The charged particles originate in thr magnestosphere and solar wind, and are directed by the Earth's magnetic field into atmosphere. Most aurora occur in a band known as the auroral zone which is typically 3◦ to 6◦ in latitudinal extent and at all local times or longitudes. Aurora is classified as diffuse and discrete aurora. The diffuse aurora is a featureless glow in the sky which may not be visible to the naked eye in a dark night and defines the extent of the auroral zone. Discrete auroras are sharply defined features within the diffuse auroras wich vary in brightness from just barely visible to the naked eye to bright enough to read newspaper at night sky because they are not as bright as the sunlit sky.
