7. hope and wish meaning??


hope itu harapan yg bisa diwujudkan & sangat mungkin terjadi

Wish adalah harapan yg sulit diwujudkan bahkan bisa dibilang mustahilwish => keinginan, hasrat. ex : she has a wish to live alone (dia punya keinginan untuk hidup sendiri). sinonim kata wish : desire (nafsu, keinginan), want (kebutuhan, kekurangan), urge (keinginan, dorongan), intention (maksud, tujuan).
hope => harapan, kesempatan. ex : i have little hope, if any (saya masih berharap, tetapi tidak terlalu yakin). sinonim : believe (percaya, kepercayaan), confidence (rahasia, kepercayaan, percaya), expectation (harapan), dream (cita2, impian).

PEMBAHASANActive-Passive VoiceActive Voice (AV) adalah kalimat aktif, yaitu Subjek melakukan tindakan/pekerjaan dalam kalimatnya.Passive Voice (PV) adalah kalimat pasif, yaitu Object yang dikenai tindakan/pekerjaan oleh Subjek dalam kalimatnya.Pola dasar Passive Voice adalah Be + V3

Contoh No.1

Rumus yg digunakan Past Tense

AV: S + V2 + O

➡ Many people saw that accident

Banyak orang melihat kecelakaan itu.Penjelasan: Kalimat ini disebut aktif karena Subjek (Many people) melakukan tindakan yaitu melihat (saw)

PV: O + Be (was/were) + V3 + By S

➡ That accident was seen by many people

Kecelakaan itu dilihat oleh banyak orangPenjelasan:Maka perubahan menjadi Passive dengan menggunakan Be Past (was/were) + Verb-3Lalu Object kecelakaan itu (that accident) dikenai tindakan yaitu dilihat (was seen) oleh subject (By Many people

Catatan Rumus Passive VoicePresent ContinuousAV: S + tobe (is/am/are) + V-ing + OPV: O + tobe (is/am/are) + being + V3 + By SPresent PerfectAV: S + has/have + V3 + OPV: O + has/have + been + V3 + By SPresent TenseAV: S + V1 (-s/es) + OPV: O + tobe (is/am/are) + V3 + By SPast PerfectAV: S + Had + V3 + OPV: O + had Been + V3 + By SFuture TenseAV: S + will + V1 + OPV: O + will be + V3 + By S

36. idiomatic expressions and their meaning and sentences


• To beat around the bush: to avoid the topic.
Example: "Stop beating around the bush. Get straight to the point, William."
Meaning: Stop avoiding the topic. Get straight to the point, William.

• To break a leg: to do one's best.
Example: "Oh, you're going to compete, Peter? Make sure to break a leg!"
Meaning: Oh, so you're going to compete, Peter? Make sure to do your best!

• To hit the shack: to go to sleep.
Example: "Darn. I'm so sleepy. Let's hit the shack, Hendrick."
Meaning: Darn, I'm so sleepy. Let's go to bed, Hendrick.

• To jump on the bandwagon: to join a popular trend.
Example: "Don't mind me, I'm just joining the bandwagon."
Meaning: "Don't mind me, I'm just doing what everyone's doing (as in, a fashion trend, etc).

• To steal someone's thunder: to take credit for something that other's did.
Example: "Have you heard? Hans is totally stealing Janshen's thunder. He didn't make the artwork, it was obvious that Janshen did!"
Meaning: Have you heard? Hans is claiming that Janshen's artwork is his's, although it's obvious that Janshen made it!"
