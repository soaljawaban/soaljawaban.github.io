1. contoh soal fisika alat optik mata kacamata dan kamera​


Jawaban:

mata

kamera

kacamata

Penjelasan:

1. Mata

Mata adalah alat optik utama yang dimiliki makhluk hidup.

2 Kamera merupakan alat yang digunakan untuk menghasilkan banyangan fotografi pada film negatif.

2. Kacamata

Kacamata merupakan alat optik yang digunakan untuk membantu melihat pada orang yang memiliki cacat mata, baik itu rabun jauh, rabun dekat, ataupun mata silindris. Kacamata terdiri dari lensa cembung atau cekung (tergantung jenis cacat matanya), frame atau kerangka yang menyangga lensa.

Kekuatan atau daya lensa dirumuskan dengan:

P = \frac{1}{f}

di mana:

P = kekuatan atau daya lensa (dioptri)

f = jarak fokus lensa (m)

Untuk mencari jarak fokus lensa, kita bisa mendapatkannya dengan menggunakan rumus:

\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{s} + \frac{1}{s'}

dimana,

s = jarak benda ke lensa (m)

s’ = jarak bayangan ke lensa (m)

7. Apa perbedaan kamera digital, kamera dslr dan kamera HD?


Kamera analog
-Masih menggunakan media pita film dalam menangkap gambar.
-Data gambar dalam bentuk filem yang harus kamu proses dulu untuk bisa mendapatkan ”foto” nya.
-kamera analog sudah hampir mampu menangkap seluruh warna yang diantulkan oleh matahari.
-kamera analog cukup sensitive.
-Kamera analog merekam dengan film negative berwarna , slide flim positif dan hitam putih.
Kamera Digital
-Menyimpan data gambarnya dalam bentuk data ”digital” yang bisa langsung kamu nikmati sesaat setelah "dijepret"
-Kamera digital belum mampu menangkap semua warna yang dipantulkan oleh matahari namun warna yang dihasilkan lebih kontras.
-Kamera digital merekam dengan pixel (picture element / elemen dasar dari film).
kamera DSLR mempunyai teknik pengambilan gambar menggunakan pentaprisma dan mechanical mirror system untuk meneruskan cahaya dari lensa menuju keoptical viewfinder pada kamera.

16. contoh teks eksplanasi tentang kamera atau fotografi dalam bahasa inggris


Jawaban:

A camera is a device that is used to capture images in the form of photographs or videos. It has been around for over a century and has evolved significantly over the years.

The basic components of a camera include a lens, an aperture, a shutter, and a sensor. The lens focuses light onto the aperture, which controls the amount of light that enters the camera. The shutter, on the other hand, controls the duration of the exposure, allowing light to hit the sensor for a specific amount of time. The sensor, also known as the image sensor, captures the light and converts it into an electrical signal, which is then processed to form an image.

Cameras can be broadly categorized into two types, film cameras and digital cameras. Film cameras use film to capture images, while digital cameras use digital sensors. Film cameras have been around for decades and are still used by some professional photographers due to the unique qualities of film. However, digital cameras have become increasingly popular due to their convenience and versatility. They allow users to view, edit and share their images immediately, and also offer greater control over image quality and exposure.

In addition to these types, there are also several different types of cameras based on the intended use, such as point-and-shoot cameras, mirrorless cameras, and DSLR cameras. Point-and-shoot cameras are small, compact cameras that are easy to use, and are perfect for everyday photography. Mirrorless cameras have similar capabilities to DSLR cameras but are smaller and more compact, making them ideal for travel and street photography. Meanwhile, DSLR cameras are larger and more advanced, and are commonly used by professional photographers.

Photography is not just about capturing images but also about capturing a moment in time, a memory, and a feeling. It's about understanding light, composition, and the technical aspect of photography. The more you learn and practice, the better photographer you become
