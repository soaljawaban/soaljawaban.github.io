Notes for the Teacher

1. The song

Do a search on the Internet to find the song “I Believe I Can Fly” by R. Kelly. When you

search, be sure to include the title and the name of the artist.

2. Song background

R. Kelly is an R & B (rhythm and blues) singer and songwriter. He first sang in subway

stations in Chicago, Illinois, but now he is very famous. In fact, he was one of the most

successful R & B performers of the 1990s.

R. Kelly first sang this song, “I Believe I Can Fly,” in 1996. R. Kelly says that he wrote

the song for Michael Jordon, a very famous basketball player who jumps very high. The

song is inspirational -- it tries to help people succeed in life.

3. Grammar background

This song uses the modal verb can to expresses ability.

Write these examples on the board. Point out to students that can is followed by the

simple form of the verb.

A bird can fly, but a cow cannot fly.

A fish can swim, but a cow can’t swim.

Could is the past tense of can. In this song, it is used to expresses ability in the past.

Write these examples on the board:

When I was a very small baby, I couldn’t talk.

I am an adult now, so I can talk.

When I was a child, I couldn’t drive a car.

I am adult now, so I can drive a car.

You can find information on can and could on pages 354 and 362 in Chapter 12 of Basic

English Grammar

How much you wanna risk? 3. I want something just like this 4. Superman unrolls 5. he lifts 6. I'm not the kind of person 7. it fits 8. I want something just like this 9. Jawaban:

1.I don't see myself upon that list

Reasons: The sentence follows the negative form of Simple Present Tense = (-) Subject + Do/Does + not + Verb-1 + Object

2. How much you wanna risk?

Reasons: The sentence follows the interrogative form of Simple Present Tense = (?) 5W1H (How much) + Do/Does + Subject + Verb-1 + Object + ?

3. I want something just like this

Reasons: The sentence follows the Positive form of Simple Present Tense = (+) Subject + Verb-1 S/ES + Object

4. Superman unrolls

Reasons: The sentence follows the Positive form of Simple Present Tense = (+) Subject + Verb-1 S/ES + Object

5. he lifts

Reasons: The sentence follows the Positive form of Simple Present Tense = (+) Subject + Verb-1 S/ES + Object

6. I'm not the kind of person

Reasons: The sentence follows the negative form of Simple Present Tense = Nominal: (-) Subject + Tobe (is/am/are) + not + Adjective / Noun / Adverb

7. it fits

Reasons: The sentence follows the Positive form of Simple Present Tense = (+) Subject + Verb-1 S/ES + Object

8. I want something just like this

Reasons: The sentence follows the Positive form of Simple Present Tense = (+) Subject + Verb-1 S/ES + Object

9. I want something just like this

Reasons: The sentence follows the Positive form of Simple Present Tense = (+) Subject + Verb-1 S/ES + Object

10. How much you wanna risk?

Reasons: The sentence follows the interrogative form of Simple Present Tense = (?) 5W1H (How much) + Do/Does + Subject + Verb-1 + Object + ?

Penjelasan:

Jawaban berdasarkan teks pada soal yang diambil dari sebuah lirik lagu terkenal dari ColdPlay and The Chainsmokers khususnya bentuk kalimat Simple Present Tense karena menjelaskan fakta dalam Bahasa Inggris.

Alasan utamanya sederhana, semua kalimat mengikuti Formula (rumus) dari bentuk kalimat Simple Present Tense.

Simple Present Tense:

Nominal: (+) Subject + Tobe (is/am/are) + Adjective / Noun / Adverb

(-) Subject + Tobe (is/am/are) + Adjective / Noun / Adverb

(?) Tobe (is/am/are) + Subject + Adjective / Noun / Adverb + ?

Verbal: (+) Subject + Verb-1 S/ES + Object

(-) Subject + Do/Does + not + Verb-1 + Object

(?) Do/Does + Subject + Verb-1 + Object + ?

(?) 5W1H (What) + Do/Does + Subject + Verb-1 + Object + ?

When I was a very small baby, I couldn’t talk.
