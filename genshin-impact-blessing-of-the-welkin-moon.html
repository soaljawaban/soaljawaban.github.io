Jawaban:

Siklus air bermanfaat untuk menjaga ketersediaan air bersih yang jumlahnya terbatas di bumi (kebanyakan air di bumi adalah air garam yang tidak bisa diminum). Siklus air mendaur ulang air secara alami, sehingga air bersih tetap tersedia di bumi.

Penjelasan:

Siklus air atau siklus hidrologi adalah sirkulasi air yang tidak pernah berhenti dari atmosfer ke bumi dan kembali ke atmosfer melalui proses kondensasi, presipitasi, evaporasi dan transpirasi

The water cycle is a crucial process that helps to maintain a balance of water on Earth. It involves the continuous journey of water from the earth's surface to the atmosphere and back, through processes like evaporation, condensation, precipitation, infiltration, and runoff. The water cycle plays a significant role in regulating the earth's temperature and climate, and impacts various aspects of human and natural ecosystems in many ways. Some of the impacts of the water cycle are as follows:

1. Availability of Water: The water cycle is responsible for the movement of freshwater that is essential for life on earth. Through the process of precipitation and infiltration, it helps to recharge groundwater levels, and replenish lakes, rivers, and oceans.

2. Weather Patterns: The water cycle is closely linked to weather patterns and climate change. It plays a critical role in the formation of clouds, precipitation, and the circulation of air masses across the earth. Changes in the water cycle can have dramatic impacts on weather patterns.

3. Agriculture: Water cycle impacts agriculture significantly. The availability of sufficient water is crucial for the growth and development of agricultural crops. Irrigation and other mechanization procedures are affected by water cycle disruptions.

4. Ecosystem Health: The water cycle plays a significant role in the health and survival of natural ecosystems. It provides freshwater to the plants, wildlife, and fishes, and helps to regulate temperature and humidity levels required to sustain them. Changes or disruptions to the water cycle may negatively impact the health of ecosystems.

5. Energy and Hydro-thermal Power: The water cycle impacts energy generation significantly. Hydroelectrical power is one of the most common sources of energy globally. The water cycle enables hydropower by providing perpetual moves required for power generation.

In conclusion, the water cycle is essential as it helps to maintain life in the terrestrial ecosystem. Therefore, ongoing monitoring of its impact and disruption prevention is necessary for sustainable temporal climate regulation, natural ecosystem health, and human well-being.

35. Analyze the impact of the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.​


The atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki by the United States in 1945 marked a significant turning point in World War II and had far-reaching consequences. The bombings led to Japan's surrender, raised ethical questions about the use of nuclear weapons, and ushered in the nuclear age.

Jawaban:

The atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki were the first and only use of nuclear weapons in war. They had a devastating impact on Japan and the world, both physically and psychologically.

the bombings killed an estimated 80,000 people in Hiroshima and 40,000 people in Nagasaki immediately, and tens of thousands more later died of radiation exposure, burns, injuries, and other effects. The bombs also destroyed most of the buildings and infrastructure in the cities, leaving behind a wasteland.

The bombings also had a political and moral impact. They forced Japan to surrender unconditionally on August 15, 1945, ending World War II in the Pacific. They also demonstrated the destructive power of nuclear weapons to the world, sparking a nuclear arms race between the United States and the Soviet Union that lasted for decades. They also raised ethical questions about the use of such weapons against civilians, and the responsibility of scientists for their inventions.

The atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki have been commemorated by survivors, peace activists, and governments ever since. They have inspired movements for nuclear disarmament, nonviolence, and human rights. They have also been the subject of many books, films, artworks, and monuments that reflect on the tragedy and its lessons for humanity.

39. How did the construction of the Berlin Wall impact the people of East Berlin?​


Jawaban:

The construction of the Berlin Wall had a significant impact on the people of East Berlin. Here are some key ways in which it affected their lives:

1. Separation of families and friends: The Berlin Wall physically divided the city of Berlin, separating families and friends who found themselves on opposite sides. Many people were suddenly cut off from their loved ones, leading to emotional distress and a loss of social connections.

2. Restriction of movement: The wall acted as a barrier, severely limiting the movement of people from East Berlin. East Germans faced strict controls and required special permits to cross into West Berlin. This restriction of movement created a sense of isolation and confinement for those living in East Berlin.

3. Economic impact: The construction of the wall created economic challenges for East Berlin. Many East Berliners relied on employment opportunities and economic ties in West Berlin, which were abruptly severed. The division hindered trade and economic growth in the East, leading to lower living standards and limited access to goods and services.

4. Suppression of dissent: The presence of the wall and the fortified border created a climate of fear and suppression of dissent in East Berlin. The East German government used the wall as a symbol of control and to prevent people from fleeing to the West. Attempts to escape were met with severe consequences, including imprisonment or even death.

5. Loss of personal freedoms: The construction of the wall further restricted personal freedoms and increased surveillance in East Berlin. The Stasi, the East German secret police, intensified their efforts to monitor and control the population, leading to a climate of mistrust and constant surveillance.

6. Psychological impact: The wall represented a physical manifestation of the ideological division between East and West during the Cold War. It served as a constant reminder of the political and ideological differences between the two sides. The presence of the wall had a psychological impact on the people of East Berlin, instilling feelings of hopelessness, frustration, and a sense of being trapped.

Overall, the construction of the Berlin Wall had a profound impact on the people of East Berlin, affecting their personal lives, relationships, freedom of movement, economic opportunities, and psychological well-being. The wall symbolized the division between East and West and served as a powerful reminder of the Cold War era and the struggles faced by those living under the communist regime.

Answer: The construction of the Berlin Wall had a significant impact on the people of East Berlin. Families and friends were separated, and it became increasingly difficult for East Berliners to visit or escape to West Berlin.
