30. At a book fair, there was an equal number of Chinese books and English books. At the end of the day, 84 Chinese books and 156 English books were sold. The ratio English books became 4:7. How many books were there altogether at the beginning of the day?​


Let's denote the number of Chinese books and English books at the beginning of the day as "C" and "E," respectively.

We know that at the end of the day, 84 Chinese books and 156 English books were sold. So, the remaining number of Chinese books and English books are (C - 84) and (E - 156), respectively.

According to the given information, the ratio of English books to Chinese books at the end of the day is 4:7. So, we can write the equation:

(E - 156) / (C - 84) = 4/7

Now, we are also given that there was an equal number of Chinese books and English books at the beginning of the day, which means C = E.

Substitute C with E in the equation:

(E - 156) / (E - 84) = 4/7

Now, let's solve this equation:

7(E - 156) = 4(E - 84)

Expand and solve for E:

7E - 1092 = 4E - 336

3E = 756

E = 252

Now that we have the value of E (the number of English books at the beginning of the day), we can find the value of C (the number of Chinese books):

C = E = 252

So, there were 252 Chinese books and 252 English books at the beginning of the day. Altogether, there were:

252 (Chinese books) + 252 (English books) = 504 books at the beginning of the day.
